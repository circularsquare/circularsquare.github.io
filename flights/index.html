what this is

Estimated passengers per year on every commercial route worldwide. Each arc is one airport-to-airport pair; brighter and thicker means more passengers.

the estimate

passengers ≈ flights × seats × 0.80. Flight counts and aircraft types are real; seat counts are a typical-per-type lookup and the 0.80 load factor is a flat assumption. It is built to compare routes, not to give exact totals.

colour & width

Both encode passengers per year. The colour ramp's steps sit at ink-weighted quantiles of the data, so the busiest routes are the brightest and no single shade floods the map. The width threshold slider sets where line width turns from linear to logarithmic — high to tell busy routes apart, low to keep thin ones visible.

top & unexpected

The right panel ranks routes two ways. top traffic is simply the busiest. unexpected finds routes carrying far more than a standard gravity model predicts from the two airports' size and distance — the diaspora corridors, migration links and historical ties this map was built to surface.

exploring

Click an airport to isolate its routes; click a route, on the map or in the scatter, to focus and zoom to it. The scatter plots every route by distance against passengers. Esc clears.

The min passengers slider hides thinner routes; flight paths, airport bubbles and projection each toggle on the left.

data

One full UTC day, 16 May 2026, annualised ×365 — so day-of-week and seasonal swings are whatever that Saturday happened to hold.

Flight data: Flightradar24. Only aggregated estimates are shown; no raw flight records.
