trancin

a fluid emitted from the ground. it was first observed in the year 924 in trapzon, when stories emerged from a high mountain monastery that an invisible, persistent current seemed to be sweeping leaves uphill.

trancin is intangible, but exerts a pull on nearby surfaces. for this reason, it stays near the surface of the ground, but it flows upward along slopes. as it flows, it can gather dust, debris, and rubble, by which the currents can be observed. this material is deposited on ridges, along which trancin streams flow and collect more strongly. this builds mountain ranges thousands of times faster than normal orogeny, and with a different shape.

if enough trancin gathers at a vertex, it can break off, carrying rocks into the sky. its unclear whether it stops going upward.

over the past 40 years  the rate of emission has grown stronger, especially in high regions of trapzon and lakta. engineers have constructed communication channels along trancin streams, sending high surface area letters along the ridges. because of this, modern cities have sprung up at these high points, such as al alta and corcyros. engineers have also constructed tranciducts to divert flow in certain beneficial directions.

over time, the mountains have gotten much higher. speed of transit is prized.
