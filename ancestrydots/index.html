Ancestry Dot Map

Each dot represents 100 reported ancestries from the US Census (ACS 5-year estimates, 2024).

You can customize colors by clicking the colored circle next to any ancestry!

Ancestry reporting in the census is voluntary. So in this data, some people are not counted, and others are double-counted (putting up to two ancestries is allowed).

* Note: The two "no reported ancestry" groups don't reflect specific census data. Rather, they are my crude estimates that attempt to count some of the people who do not report ancestry. White, no reported ancestry is calculated per census tract as the white (race) population minus all the ancestries in the Eastern European, Western European, and North American groups. Black, no reported ancestry is analogously the black (race) population minus the African and Afro-Caribbean groups.

Dots are distributed randomly throughout their census tract's land area.
